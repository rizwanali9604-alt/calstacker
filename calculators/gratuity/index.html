Frequently Asked Questions

For employees covered under the Payment of Gratuity Act, gratuity = (Last drawn Basic + DA) × 15/26 × Number of years of service. The factor 15/26 represents 15 days' wages for each completed year.
Gratuity received by government employees is fully exempt. For private sector employees, exemption is available up to ₹20 lakh under Section 10(10) of the Income Tax Act. Amount above this limit is taxable.

You must have completed at least 5 years of continuous service with the same employer (except in case of death or disablement). Gratuity is typically paid on resignation, retirement, or superannuation.
Yes. Dearness Allowance (DA) is included along with basic salary in the "last drawn salary" used for gratuity computation under the Act.
