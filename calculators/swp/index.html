Frequently Asked Questions

SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) lets you withdraw a fixed amount from your mutual fund investment at regular intervals — ideal for retirees needing steady income.
SWP from debt or hybrid funds can offer better post-tax returns than FDs for those in lower tax brackets. However, market-linked returns carry some volatility risk.
It depends on withdrawal amount, return rate, and initial corpus. If monthly returns exceed withdrawals, the corpus can last indefinitely. This calculator shows the projected timeline.
Each SWP withdrawal is treated as redemption. Capital gains tax applies based on fund type and holding period. Only the gain portion is taxed, not the full withdrawal.
A common rule is withdrawing 4–6% of corpus annually (roughly 0.33–0.5% monthly). Higher rates deplete corpus faster, especially in low-return environments.
