Frequently Asked Questions

Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal amount throughout the loan or deposit period. The interest earned does not get reinvested or compounded.
SI = P × R × T / 100, where P is principal, R is annual rate of interest (%), and T is time in years. Total amount = P + SI.
Simple interest is commonly used for short-term personal loans, car loans, some government schemes, and educational loans. Most bank deposits use compound interest instead.
Simple interest grows linearly; compound interest grows exponentially because interest is reinvested. Over long periods, compound interest significantly outperforms simple interest.
Yes. Convert months to years by dividing by 12. For example, 18 months = 1.5 years. Enter decimal values in the time field for partial years.
