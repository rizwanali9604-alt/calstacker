Frequently Asked Questions

Corpus depends on your lifestyle expenses, inflation, and retirement duration. We calculate the amount needed to sustain inflation-adjusted monthly expenses for 25 years post-retirement using your return assumptions.
₹50,000/month today won't buy the same in 30 years. At 6% inflation, expenses roughly quadruple over 25 years. Ignoring inflation leads to severe under-saving.
For long-term equity-heavy portfolios, 10–12% is common. Conservative planners use 8–10%. Post-retirement, many shift to 7–8% in balanced or debt-heavy portfolios.
NPS offers tax benefits under Section 80CCD and low fund management costs. However, 60% of corpus must be annuitised at maturity. Many combine NPS with mutual fund SIPs.
The earlier the better. Starting a SIP in your 20s or 30s leverages compounding over decades. Even small monthly amounts grow significantly over 25–30 years.
