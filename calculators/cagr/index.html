Frequently Asked Questions

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is the mean annual growth rate of an investment over a specified period, assuming profits are reinvested each year. It smooths out volatility.
CAGR = (Ending Value / Beginning Value)1/n − 1, where n is the number of years. Multiply by 100 to express as a percentage.
Absolute return shows total gain over the period. CAGR annualises that return, making it easier to compare investments held for different durations.
Yes. If your final value is less than the initial value, CAGR will be negative, indicating an average annual loss over the investment period.
Standard CAGR works best for lumpsum investments. For SIPs with multiple cash flows, use XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return) for a more accurate measure.
