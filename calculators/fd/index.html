Understanding Fd in India

Fixed Deposits (FD) are bank deposits with guaranteed returns for a fixed tenure. Senior citizens often get 0.25–0.50% extra. SBI, HDFC, ICICI FD rates for 1–5 years typically range 6.5–7.5% in 2026. Interest is fully taxable at your slab rate. TDS applies if interest exceeds ₹40,000/year (₹50,000 for seniors).

Formula

Maturity = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t) for compound FD, or P + P×r×t for simple FD structures. Most bank FDs compound quarterly.

Worked Example

₹5 lakh FD at 7% for 5 years compounded quarterly: maturity ≈ ₹7.07 lakh, interest ≈ ₹2.07 lakh before tax. At 30% tax slab, post-tax interest ≈ ₹1.45 lakh.

Key Factors That Affect Your Result

  • Tenure — longer often higher rate up to a point
  • Senior citizen rates — typically +0.50%
  • Tax on interest — no 80C unless 5-year tax-saver FD
  • Premature withdrawal — penalty of 0.5–1% on rate
  • DICGC insurance — deposits insured up to ₹5 lakh per bank
