Frequently Asked Questions

Inflation is the rate at which the general price level of goods and services rises, reducing the purchasing power of money over time. India's long-term average is 5–6%.
Money sitting in a savings account earning 3–4% loses real value when inflation is 6%. ₹1 lakh today may only buy goods worth ₹55,000 in 10 years at 6% inflation.
Purchasing power loss is the difference between today's value and what the same amount will be worth in the future. It shows how much "real value" inflation erodes from your money.
Invest in assets that historically outpace inflation: equity mutual funds (12%+ long-term), real estate, gold, and PPF. Avoid keeping large sums in low-interest savings accounts.
For long-term planning in India, 5–6% is a reasonable assumption based on RBI's target and historical CPI data. For conservative planning, use 7–8%.
