Understanding Gst in India

Goods and Services Tax (GST) in India has four main slabs: 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%, plus 0% and 3% for precious metals. Most services attract 18% GST. Businesses above ₹40 lakh turnover (₹20 lakh in special category states) must register. GSTIN is required for B2B invoicing and input tax credit claims.

Formula

GST Amount = Taxable Value × Rate / 100. Total = Taxable Value + GST. For reverse: Base = Total / (1 + Rate/100).

Worked Example

Product price ₹10,000 + 18% GST: GST = ₹1,800, total = ₹11,800. Inclusive pricing ₹11,800: base = ₹10,000, GST = ₹1,800.

Key Factors That Affect Your Result

  • CGST + SGST — split equally for intra-state sales
  • IGST — inter-state transactions
  • Input tax credit — offset GST paid on purchases
  • Composition scheme — simplified compliance for small business
  • HSN/SAC codes — determine applicable rate
