Frequently Asked Questions

Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal and accumulated interest from previous periods. It makes your money grow faster than simple interest.
More frequent compounding (monthly vs yearly) yields slightly higher returns for the same nominal rate. Daily compounding gives the highest effective return.
A = P(1 + r/n)nt, where P is principal, r is annual rate (decimal), n is compounding periods per year, and t is time in years.
Simple interest is calculated only on principal (SI = P×R×T/100). Compound interest reinvests earned interest, leading to exponential growth over long periods.
Fixed deposits, PPF, EPF, mutual fund SIPs, and most savings products use compound interest. Bank FDs typically compound quarterly in India.
