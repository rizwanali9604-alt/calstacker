Frequently Asked Questions

A lumpsum investment is a one-time payment into a mutual fund, stock, or fixed deposit. Unlike SIP, you invest the entire amount at once and let compounding work over time.
We use the compound interest formula A = P(1+r)t, where P is principal, r is annual return rate (decimal), and t is years. Returns are compounded annually.
Lumpsum works best when markets are low and you have a large amount ready. SIP reduces timing risk through rupee-cost averaging. Many investors combine both strategies.
