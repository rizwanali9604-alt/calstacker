Frequently Asked Questions

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) lets you invest a fixed amount in a mutual fund at regular intervals—usually monthly. It builds discipline and benefits from rupee-cost averaging over time.
M = P × ((1+i)^n − 1) / i × (1+i), where P is monthly investment, i is monthly return (annual ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is total months. This is the standard future-value formula for SIPs with end-of-month contributions.
Equity mutual funds have historically delivered around 10–14% CAGR over long periods, but past performance does not guarantee future returns. Debt funds offer lower but steadier returns. Adjust the rate to match your fund category.
Most mutual funds in India allow SIPs starting from ₹500 per month. Some funds have higher minimums. Check the fund's offer document on the AMC website.
