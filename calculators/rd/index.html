Frequently Asked Questions

An RD is a bank deposit where you invest a fixed amount every month for a fixed tenure. Interest is compounded quarterly, similar to FDs but with monthly contributions.
Indian banks use M = R × [(1+i)n − 1] / (1 − (1+i)−1/3) where i = rate/400 (quarterly rate) and n = number of quarters in the tenure.
RDs offer guaranteed returns and are ideal for risk-averse savers. SIPs in equity mutual funds offer higher potential returns but with market risk. Many use both.
