Frequently Asked Questions

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is a fixed payment you make each month toward your loan. It includes both principal repayment and interest, calculated so the loan is fully paid off by the end of the tenure.
EMI = P × R × (1+R)^N / ((1+R)^N − 1), where P is principal, R is monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and N is tenure in months. This is the standard reducing-balance method used by Indian banks.
