EPF Complete Guide India 2026 — Everything About Provident Fund

Contribution rules, balance checks, UAN activation, withdrawal conditions, and a real ₹20,000 basic salary example.

Published: 8 June 2026

The Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) is one of the most important long-term savings instruments for salaried workers in India. Managed by the Employees' Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO), it builds a retirement corpus through mandatory monthly contributions from both you and your employer, backed by a government-declared interest rate. For millions of employees, EPF represents the single largest financial asset they will accumulate over a working career — yet many people do not fully understand how contributions are split, how to track their balance, or when they can withdraw funds. This guide covers everything you need to know about EPF in 2026.

What Is EPF and Who Must Contribute?

EPF is a social security scheme established under the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952. It applies to factories and establishments that employ 20 or more employees. Once an organisation crosses this threshold, it must register with EPFO and deduct provident fund from eligible employees' salaries every month.

All employees earning a basic salary plus dearness allowance (DA) of up to ₹15,000 per month must be enrolled mandatorily. Employers can extend EPF coverage to employees earning above ₹15,000 basic — either on actual salary or on a voluntary higher contribution basis. Even establishments with fewer than 20 employees may register voluntarily with EPFO. If you receive a salary slip showing PF deductions, you are almost certainly covered under this scheme.

EPF Contribution Rates Explained

Both employee and employer each contribute 12% of basic salary plus DA toward provident fund benefits. However, the employer's 12% is not deposited entirely into your EPF account. It is split across two schemes:

  • Employee share (12%): Deducted from your salary and credited fully to your EPF account.
  • Employer EPF share (3.67%): Credited to your EPF account alongside your contribution.
  • Employer EPS share (8.33%): Diverted to the Employees' Pension Scheme (EPS). This builds your pension entitlement, not your EPF balance.

For EPS, the 8.33% is calculated on a maximum wage ceiling of ₹15,000 per month — meaning the maximum EPS contribution is ₹1,249.50 per month regardless of how high your basic salary is. The remaining portion of the employer's 12% (after EPS) flows into your EPF account. Additionally, employers pay 0.50% as EDLI (Employees' Deposit Linked Insurance) and 0.50% as EPF administrative charges on capped wages.

Many employers calculate statutory PF on the ₹15,000 wage ceiling even when your basic exceeds that amount. This means your visible PF deduction may be ₹1,800 per month (12% of ₹15,000) rather than 12% of your full basic. Check your offer letter and salary slip to confirm whether your employer deducts on actual basic or the statutory cap.

EPF Interest Rate for FY 2025-26

The EPFO board has declared an interest rate of 8.25% per annum for FY 2025-26. This rate applies to the accumulated balance in your EPF account and is among the highest available on a government-backed, relatively low-risk instrument. Interest is calculated on the monthly running balance and credited once a year after approval by the Ministry of Finance.

Unlike fixed deposits where interest is paid out periodically, EPF interest stays in your account and compounds over time. The rate is reviewed annually and depends on EPFO's investment performance across government securities and approved instruments. For long-term planners, even a difference of 0.25% over 20 years can meaningfully change your final corpus, so it is worth tracking the declared rate each year.

How to Check Your EPF Balance

EPFO offers several convenient methods to check your provident fund balance without visiting a regional office:

  • EPFO Member Portal: Log in at unifiedportal-mem.epfindia.gov.in using your activated UAN and password. Navigate to "View Passbook" to see month-wise credits, employer contributions, and interest postings for each member ID linked to your UAN.
  • UMANG App: Download the UMANG (Unified Mobile Application for New-age Governance) app, search for EPFO, and access passbook, claim status, and UAN services from your phone.
  • Missed Call Service: Give a missed call to 011-22901406 from your UAN-registered mobile number. You will receive an SMS with your latest PF balance details within a few minutes.
  • SMS Service: Send an SMS in the format EPFOHO UAN ENG to 7738299899 from your registered mobile. Replace ENG with your preferred language code (HIN for Hindi, etc.).

For all these services, your UAN must be activated and your mobile number must be linked and verified. If you recently changed jobs, ensure your old PF account has been transferred to your current UAN so the passbook reflects your complete balance.

UAN Activation — Step by Step

The Universal Account Number (UAN) is a 12-digit identifier that stays with you throughout your career, even when you change employers. Every new PF account opened by subsequent employers gets linked to the same UAN, making transfers and withdrawals far simpler.

To activate your UAN for the first time, visit the EPFO member portal and select "Activate UAN." Enter your UAN (found on your salary slip), PF member ID, name, date of birth, and mobile number. Verify with the OTP sent to your phone, then set a login password. Complete e-KYC by linking your Aadhaar, PAN, and bank account details — this is essential for online withdrawal claims to process without delays. If your employer has not communicated your UAN, request it from the HR or payroll team; they receive it from EPFO at the time of your enrollment.

EPF Withdrawal Rules in 2026

EPF is designed as a retirement savings vehicle, so full withdrawal before retirement is restricted. Here are the main scenarios:

  • Retirement: Full withdrawal is permitted at age 58 (or the retirement age specified by your employer).
  • Unemployment: You can withdraw 75% of your balance after one month of unemployment, and the remaining 25% after two months without rejoining EPF-covered employment.
  • Partial withdrawal: Allowed for specific life events — home purchase or construction (after 5 years of service), medical emergencies, marriage, higher education, and loan repayment — subject to amount limits defined by EPFO.
  • Transfer, not withdrawal: When changing jobs, you should transfer your old PF balance to the new employer's account via the UAN-linked online transfer claim (Form 13). This preserves continuous service for tax exemption and pension eligibility.

Online claims submitted through the EPFO portal or UMANG app with complete KYC are typically processed within 7 to 20 working days. Offline claims through your employer take longer.

Real Scenario: ₹20,000 Basic Salary for 10 Years

Consider an employee with a monthly basic salary of ₹20,000 (no DA), where the employer deducts PF on the statutory ₹15,000 wage ceiling — the most common arrangement in India.

  • Employee contribution: 12% × ₹15,000 = ₹1,800/month
  • Employer EPF contribution: 3.67% × ₹15,000 = ₹550.50/month
  • Total monthly EPF credit: ₹2,350.50
  • Employer EPS contribution (not in EPF balance): 8.33% × ₹15,000 = ₹1,249.50/month

Over 10 years (120 months), total contributions to the EPF account equal approximately ₹2.82 lakh. At the current interest rate of 8.25% compounded monthly, the accumulated EPF balance grows to roughly ₹4.36 lakh — meaning about ₹1.54 lakh comes from interest alone. If the same employer instead contributed on the full ₹20,000 basic, the monthly EPF credit would rise to ₹3,134 and the 10-year corpus would reach approximately ₹5.81 lakh. 4 Common EPF Mistakes to Avoid

1. Not transferring PF when changing jobs. Leaving an old PF account dormant means your balance earns interest but your service period appears broken. Transfer via UAN immediately after joining a new employer to maintain continuous service for tax-free withdrawal and pension benefits.

2. Ignoring UAN activation and KYC. Without an activated UAN linked to Aadhaar, PAN, and bank account, online withdrawals and passbook access are blocked. Many employees discover this only when they urgently need funds.

3. Withdrawing EPF on every job change. Tempting as it may be, early withdrawal before five years of continuous service triggers tax liability and destroys compounding. EPF works best when left untouched for decades.

4. Assuming 12% employer share goes entirely to EPF. Only 3.67% of employer contribution lands in your EPF passbook. The 8.33% EPS portion funds your pension — check your EPS service history separately if you plan to claim a pension at age 58.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is EPF mandatory for all employees in India?

EPF is mandatory for establishments with 20 or more employees under the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952. Once registered, all eligible employees earning up to ₹15,000 basic wages must be enrolled. Employers may extend coverage to employees above this threshold voluntarily. Even if your company has fewer than 20 employees, it can register voluntarily. Contract and temporary workers may also be covered depending on employer policy and EPFO classification rules.

What is the current EPF interest rate for FY 2025-26?

The EPFO declared 8.25% per annum as the EPF interest rate for FY 2025-26. Interest is credited to your account once a year, typically after the finance ministry approves the rate. It is calculated on the monthly running balance and compounded monthly in practice. The rate is reviewed annually by the EPFO board and may change based on the fund's investment returns. For planning purposes, treat 8.25% as the current benchmark but monitor EPFO announcements each financial year.

How do I activate my UAN for the first time?

Your employer generates a Universal Account Number (UAN) when you join EPF. To activate it, visit the EPFO member portal at unifiedportal-mem.epfindia.gov.in and click "Activate UAN". Enter your UAN, mobile number, and member ID from your salary slip. You will receive an OTP on your registered mobile. Set a password and complete KYC by linking your Aadhaar, PAN, and bank account. Once activated, you can view passbooks, submit transfer claims, and track withdrawals from a single dashboard across all employers.

When can I withdraw my full EPF balance?

Full EPF withdrawal is allowed at retirement (typically age 58), two months after leaving employment if you remain unemployed, or upon permanent disability. Partial withdrawals are permitted for specific purposes such as home purchase or construction, medical treatment, marriage, education, and loan repayment — each with minimum service requirements and amount caps. Online claims through the EPFO portal or UMANG app are processed faster when your UAN, Aadhaar, and bank details are fully KYC-verified.

Is EPF withdrawal tax-free in India?

EPF withdrawal is tax-exempt under Section 10(12) if you have completed five years of continuous service, including service with previous employers after a proper transfer. Withdrawals before five years are taxable, and TDS may apply if the amount exceeds ₹50,000 and your PAN is not linked. Additionally, interest earned on employee contributions exceeding ₹2.5 lakh per year (₹5 lakh for government employees) is taxable from FY 2021-22 onwards. Always keep your UAN linked and transfer old PF accounts to preserve continuous service.
